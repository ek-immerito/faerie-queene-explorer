# Textual source and methodology

## Source edition

The poem text in the Faerie Queene Explorer derives from:

Edmund Spenser, *Spenser’s Faerie Queene*, edited by J. C. Smith, 2 vols. (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1909).

The electronic transcriptions were provided by Project Gutenberg:

- [Project Gutenberg eBook 70717](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/70717): Books I–III
- [Project Gutenberg eBook 72698](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/72698): Books IV–VII, including the *Mutabilitie Cantos*

The source downloads include Project Gutenberg’s transcriber’s notes and license information. Project Gutenberg is not responsible for subsequent processing, corrections, search normalization, thematic indexing, or interface design in the Faerie Queene Explorer.

## Explorer processing

The Explorer parses the source transcription into Book, Canto, stanza, and line records. It adds citation navigation, full-text and regular-expression searching, controlled spelling and morphological families, early-modern orthographic and ligature normalization, character and thematic indexes, KWIC display, filtering, charts, and exports.

Normalized search affects discovery and highlighting only. It does not silently modernize the displayed poem text. Exact and regular-expression modes remain available for checking original forms.

## Documented transcription correction

Project Gutenberg eBook 70717 repeats the stanza number `lxiv` immediately after
`lxxiii` in Book II, Canto X. The latter occurrence is treated here as `lxxiv`,
following the uninterrupted stanza sequence and preventing two distinct passages
from sharing the citation II.x.64. The verse itself has not been altered.

## Editorial caution

The Explorer is a research aid rather than a new critical edition. Automated parsing and normalization can introduce omissions, false positives, or structural errors. Researchers should verify consequential quotations and textual claims against the Project Gutenberg transcription and, where appropriate, the Smith edition or early printed witnesses.

## Reporting errors

When reporting an error, include the Explorer citation, the displayed line, the expected reading, and the source used for comparison.
